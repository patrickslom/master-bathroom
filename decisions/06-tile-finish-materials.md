# Tile and Finish Materials

## Outcome

Select a compatible, durable finish package after the underlying construction systems are defined.

## Decisions Required

- What tile will be used on the main floor, shower walls, and shower floor?
- What tile sizes and layouts work with the room, slope, drain, and substrate?
- What grout type, joint size, and color will be used?
- What movement-joint sealant is required?
- What wall paint and trim finish will be used?
- Which visible metal finish will coordinate plumbing, lighting, and hardware?

## Current Direction

- Light, modern, timeless, and visually enlarging
- Porcelain tile selected for the main bathroom floor
- Light large-format main floor tile
- Porcelain mosaic shower floor for slope and grip
- Epoxy or another high-performance grout for wet areas
- Black or soft gold / champagne bronze hardware direction
- A clean painted vanity wall is the baseline direction
- A subtle accent treatment behind the mirrors remains an option

## Timeless Design Guardrails

- Use neutral colors, subtle texture, classic patterns, and clean lines
- Avoid excessive visual clutter or loud, highly trend-driven treatments
- An accent wall may be used if it remains subtle and coordinated
- Moisture-rated wallpaper is acceptable behind the mirrors if ventilation is effective and the wall is not directly soaked

## Recommended MVP

Use a restrained package with one main floor tile, one shower wall tile, one shower-floor mosaic, and one coordinated metal finish. Avoid decorative patterns that increase labor, waste, or waterproofing complexity.

## Dependencies

- Measurements and quantities
- Shower system and drain
- Plumbing and lighting fixture finishes

## MVP Exit Criteria

- [ ] All tile models, sizes, quantities, and layouts selected
- [ ] Shower-floor tile confirmed compatible with slope and drain
- [ ] Mortar, grout, and sealants confirmed compatible with substrates and waterproofing
- [ ] Waste factor and spare-material quantities included
- [ ] Physical samples reviewed together in the bathroom lighting
