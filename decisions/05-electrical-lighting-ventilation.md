# Electrical, Lighting, and Ventilation

## Outcome

Provide safe electrical service, useful lighting, and enough ventilation to manage bathroom moisture.

## Decisions Required

- Is the existing exhaust fan correctly sized and vented outdoors?
- Will the fan be replaced, relocated, or controlled by a timer or humidity sensor?
- Are new circuits, GFCI protection, switches, or receptacles required?
- What vanity, ceiling, and shower lighting will be installed?
- What color temperature and brightness should the lighting provide?
- Do fixture locations need to move before walls are closed?

## Recommended MVP

- Treat effective outdoor-vented exhaust as required, not decorative
- Keep lighting locations when they work, but correct poor vanity illumination
- Use simple, readily replaceable fixtures and controls

## Confirmed Ventilation Requirements

- Install or upgrade to a properly sized bathroom exhaust fan
- Exhaust humid air outdoors, never into an attic or wall cavity
- Run the fan during showers and afterward
- Do not rely on the skylight / sun tunnel for moisture removal
- The fan must pull air out of the bathroom

## Current Ventilation Direction

- Locate the fan near the shower
- Direct exterior-wall venting is preferred when practical
- Prefer a short, direct run of rigid ducting with a properly sealed exterior hood
- Size the fan using bathroom area, ceiling height, duct length, and shower use
- Use roughly 1 CFM per square foot only as an initial sizing guideline
- Consider a second fan only if room size or poor airflow justifies it
- A fan directly over the shower is acceptable only when appropriately rated and installed with required electrical protection

## Dependencies

- Measurements and layout
- Mirror and vanity plan
- Applicable permit and electrical requirements

## MVP Exit Criteria

- [ ] Ventilation capacity and outdoor termination confirmed
- [ ] Fan and control strategy selected
- [ ] Lighting and switching plan documented
- [ ] Electrical rough-in changes documented
- [ ] Required licensed work and inspections confirmed
