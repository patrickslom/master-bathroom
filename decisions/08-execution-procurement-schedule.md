# Execution, Procurement, and Schedule

## Outcome

Convert approved decisions into a build sequence with responsible people, available materials, checkpoints, and cost controls.

## Decisions Required

- Who owns each work package and inspection?
- What must be purchased before demolition?
- Which products have long lead times?
- Where will materials be stored and inspected?
- What is the planned construction sequence?
- What conditions require work to stop for a decision?
- How will changes, costs, receipts, warranties, and progress photos be tracked?

## Recommended MVP

- Do not begin demolition until verticals 1 through 3 are complete
- Do not close walls until rough-in work is tested and approved
- Do not install tile until waterproofing passes its flood test
- Purchase critical-system components before decorative products
- Keep contingency funds uncommitted until hidden conditions are known

## Required Hold Points

1. After demolition: inspect insulation, framing, plumbing, and subfloor
2. Before closing walls: verify rough-ins and required inspections
3. Before tile: inspect waterproofing and complete flood testing
4. Before final payment or completion: verify drainage, leaks, finishes, and punch-list items

## MVP Exit Criteria

- [ ] Responsibility matrix completed
- [ ] Procurement list and lead times documented
- [ ] Build sequence and hold points agreed
- [ ] Materials available before their installation dates
- [ ] Budget tracking and change-approval process established
