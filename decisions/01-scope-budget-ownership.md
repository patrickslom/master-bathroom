# Scope, Budget, and Ownership

## Outcome

Define exactly what this remodel includes, how much can be spent, and who is responsible for each type of work.

## Decisions Required

- Is this a full bathroom remodel or primarily a shower remodel plus cosmetic updates?
- Which existing items will remain, be refinished, or be replaced?
- Will the shower footprint or bathroom layout change?
- What work will be DIY, contractor-led, or completed by licensed trades?
- What is the target budget?
- What contingency is reserved for hidden damage discovered during demolition?
- What permits, inspections, or local requirements apply?
- What improvements can be deferred without causing later rework?

## Current Direction

- Complete a full bathroom remodel
- Compare keeping the existing black granite vanity countertop against a practical quartz or granite remnant replacement
- Remove the existing granite shower bench if practical
- Refresh the full bathroom with a lighter, cleaner, timeless design
- Prioritize waterproofing, durability, ventilation, and easy cleaning
- Patrick and Melissa, the homeowners, plan to perform all work themselves
- Build the working budget from documented material prices and add a 10% buffer

## Recommended MVP

- Keep plumbing locations and the general bathroom layout unless measurements reveal a strong reason to change them
- Complete all shower reconstruction, waterproofing, required repairs, and ventilation work correctly
- Limit decorative upgrades if needed to protect the budget for hidden-condition repairs
- Reserve a meaningful contingency before demolition

## MVP Exit Criteria

- [ ] Written in-scope and out-of-scope lists
- [x] Budget method and contingency approved
- [x] DIY versus contractor responsibilities assigned
- [ ] Permit and inspection requirements understood
- [ ] Explicit agreement on which improvements may be deferred

## Decisions

| Decision | Selection | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Project scope | Full bathroom remodel | Includes shower, main floor, vanity area, lighting, ventilation, walls, and finishes |
| Target budget | Calculate after material selections | Document provided material prices and maintain a running total |
| Contingency | 10% buffer | Add 10% to the documented material-cost total |
| DIY work | All project work | Patrick and Melissa are skilled homeowners and plan to perform all work |
| Contractor work | None planned | Reconsider only if an unexpected condition exceeds the DIY scope |
| Licensed-trade work | None planned | Verify local permit and licensing requirements before applicable work |

## Budget Method

1. Record material selections and prices as decisions are made.
2. Calculate the current material subtotal.
3. Add a 10% buffer to the subtotal.
4. Track actual purchases against the calculated total.
5. Recalculate whenever scope or material selections change.
