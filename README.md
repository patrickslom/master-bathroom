# Master Bathroom Remodel

Planning and project documentation for Melissa and Patrick's master bathroom remodel.

This repo is meant to keep the remodel organized in one place: scope, design choices, measurements, materials, budget, schedule, decisions, and progress photos.

## Current Status

**Phase:** Planning / design direction  
**Main goal:** Full bathroom refresh with a lighter, cleaner, timeless look while keeping the existing black granite vanity top.

## Project Goals

- Create a bathroom that feels lighter, bigger, airy, modern, and timeless
- Keep the existing black granite countertop
- Replace or update everything else: sinks, faucets, mirrors, lighting, tile, walls, shower finishes, and hardware
- Use finishes that are durable, easy to clean, and not overly trendy
- Document all measurements, products, prices, decisions, and progress
- Track budget and schedule before demo begins

## Current Design Direction

### Overall Style

Current target style:

- Bright, airy, and clean
- Soft minimal / Scandinavian-inspired
- Hotel-like, but not cold
- Timeless but modern
- White, ivory, light greige, and soft neutral colors
- Simple lines, light materials, and calm finishes
- Avoid anything that looks too plastic, cheap, busy, or overly trendy

## Existing Items We Are Keeping

### Vanity Countertop

- Keep the existing black granite countertop.
- Do not modify the color of the countertop.
- The granite backsplash can be removed if it improves the final design because it is not permanently fixed to the countertop.

### Vanity Layout

- Keep the general double-vanity layout.
- Use vessel sinks that sit on top of the existing countertop because of the existing cutouts.
- Preferred sink color: white.
- Preferred sink shape: rectangular or softly squared, not round.

## Vanity / Countertop Area Decisions

### Cabinet

Current decision:

- Cabinet should be white.
- Style should stay clean and timeless, similar to shaker-style doors/drawers.
- The white cabinet keeps the vanity bright so the black granite countertop feels intentional instead of heavy.

### Hardware

Current decision:

- Use matte black hardware throughout.
- This includes vanity pulls, knobs, faucets, mirror frames, towel rings, shower glass hardware, shower valve trim, shower head, handheld sprayer, and drain cover if available.
- Black hardware works with the black granite countertop and helps tie the vanity and shower together.

Design note:

- Black hardware is modern-classic rather than the most traditional timeless finish.
- It should work well here because the countertop is already black.
- Keep the room very bright everywhere else so the black accents feel balanced and intentional.

### Mirrors

Current direction:

- Use tall mirrors with simple black frames.
- Rounded rectangle mirrors are preferred because they soften the room while staying modern.

### Vanity Lighting

Current decision:

- Use black horizontal bar lights above the mirrors.
- Keep the lights simple, modern, and minimal.
- Use warm white lighting, approximately 2700K–3000K.
- Avoid overly decorative fixtures.

## Bathroom Floor Decisions

Current direction:

- Use large-format bathroom floor tiles.
- Do not use small mosaic tile for the main bathroom floor.
- Floor should be light, bright, and airy.
- Preferred tones: warm ivory, light greige, soft beige, or light stone-look porcelain.
- Matte or low-sheen finish preferred for safety and a softer look.
- Minimal grout lines where possible.

Design note:

- It is common to match the shower wall tile and bathroom floor tile, but the current preferred direction is to coordinate them instead of making everything exactly the same.
- Coordinating materials should make the room feel connected without looking flat or like a showroom box.

## Shower Design Decisions

### Overall Shower Look

Current direction:

- We love the current shower design direction.
- Keep the shower bright, clean, modern, and spa-like.
- Use a frameless glass shower enclosure.
- Use matte black shower hardware to match the vanity area.
- Remove the existing granite shower seat / bench if possible.
- Avoid finishes that look like plastic.

### Shower Wall Tile

Current decision:

- Use large-format bright porcelain tiles on the shower walls.
- Preferred look: soft white or warm ivory porcelain.
- Finish should be matte or satin, not overly glossy.
- Pattern should be subtle, such as light stone movement or soft marble-like veining.
- Avoid busy or high-contrast veining.

### Shower Floor Tile

Current direction:

- Shower floor should coordinate with the shower walls and bathroom floor.
- Because of slope and grip, use a smaller tile or mosaic on the shower floor.
- Keep the color family light and soft.
- Porcelain mosaic remains the preferred shower floor direction.

### Shower Niche

Current direction:

- Include a long recessed shower niche.
- Add subtle built-in lighting if practical.
- Keep the niche clean and minimal.

### Shower Hardware

Current decision:

- Use matte black shower hardware.
- Black frameless glass hardware / rail.
- Black shower head.
- Black valve trim.
- Black handheld sprayer.
- Black drain cover if available.

## Material Coordination Strategy

The current plan is to coordinate materials instead of matching everything exactly.

Recommended approach:

- Shower walls: large bright porcelain tile in soft white or warm ivory.
- Bathroom floor: large-format light greige, warm ivory, soft beige, or light stone-look porcelain.
- Shower floor: smaller mosaic in the same general color family for grip and slope.
- Vanity: white cabinet with existing black granite countertop.
- Sinks: white vessel sinks.
- Hardware: matte black throughout.
- Lighting: black horizontal bar lights above mirrors.

This should create one connected style while keeping the bathroom bright, airy, modern, and timeless.

## Project Phases

### 1. Planning

- Confirm design direction
- Take measurements
- Select materials
- Confirm budget
- Decide what work is DIY vs contractor
- Order long-lead items

### 2. Demo

- Empty bathroom
- Protect surrounding areas
- Remove old fixtures, mirrors, lighting, and finishes
- Remove vanity items carefully if anything is being reused
- Open walls/floor only as needed
- Inspect for water damage or hidden issues

### 3. Rough-In / Prep

- Plumbing adjustments
- Electrical and lighting updates
- Ventilation upgrades if needed
- Wall repair or replacement
- Shower waterproofing system
- Floor prep and leveling if needed

### 4. Tile and Finish Work

- Floor tile installation
- Shower tile installation
- Grout
- Paint
- Install sinks, faucets, hardware, mirrors, and lights

### 5. Final Review

- Check for leaks
- Confirm drainage and shower slope
- Seal anything that requires sealing
- Touch up paint and caulk
- Final clean
- Add final photos and notes
