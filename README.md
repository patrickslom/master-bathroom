# Master Bathroom Remodel

Planning and project documentation for Melissa and Patrick's master bathroom remodel.

This repo is meant to keep the remodel organized in one place: scope, design choices, measurements, materials, budget, schedule, decisions, and progress photos.

## Current Status

**Phase:** Planning / design direction  
**Main goal:** Full bathroom refresh with a lighter, cleaner, timeless look while keeping the existing black granite vanity top.

## Project Goals

- Create a bathroom that feels lighter, bigger, airy, modern, and timeless
- Keep the existing black granite countertop if possible
- Replace or update everything else: sinks, faucets, mirrors, lighting, tile, walls, shower finishes, and hardware
- Use finishes that are durable, easy to clean, and not overly trendy
- Document all measurements, products, prices, decisions, and progress
- Track budget and schedule before demo begins

## Design Direction

### Overall Style

- Light, clean, modern, timeless
- White or light walls
- Lighter floor tile to make the room feel larger
- Warm but simple finishes
- Avoid anything that looks too plastic, cheap, or overly trendy

### Vanity Area

**Keeping:**

- Existing black granite countertop

**Changing / updating:**

- Cabinet color may be repainted to better match the black granite and lighter room design
- Vessel sinks are required because of the existing counter cutouts
- Preferred sink color: white
- Preferred sink shape: not round; consider rectangular, soft-rectangle, oval, or modern organic shapes
- New mirrors
- New lighting
- New faucets
- New hardware

**Hardware finish options:**

- Black
- Soft gold / champagne bronze

### Floor Tile

Goal: light, timeless, modern, and visually enlarging.

Good directions to consider:

- Large-format light porcelain tile
- Warm white, ivory, light beige, greige, or soft stone-look tile
- Matte or low-sheen finish for safety and a softer look
- Minimal grout lines where possible

### Shower Area

Current direction:

- Remodel the shower to feel lighter, modern, and timeless
- Remove the existing granite shower seat / bench if possible
- Avoid finishes that look like plastic
- Porcelain tile is preferred for durability and long-term appearance
- Shower floor direction: porcelain mosaic tile for better slope and grip

Important shower priorities:

- Proper waterproofing behind the tile
- Good ventilation to reduce moisture, mildew, and cleaning issues
- Durable grout selection
- Easy-to-clean finishes

### Grout Direction

Preferred direction:

- Epoxy grout or another high-performance grout should be considered for the shower floor and wet areas

Why:

- Better stain resistance
- Better water resistance
- Less maintenance than basic cement grout
- Helps reduce mold and mildew issues when paired with good ventilation and cleaning

## Project Phases

### 1. Planning

- Confirm design direction
- Take measurements
- Select materials
- Confirm budget
- Decide what work is DIY vs contractor
- Order long-lead items

### 2. Demo

- Empty bathroom
- Protect surrounding areas
- Remove old fixtures, mirrors, lighting, and finishes
- Remove vanity items carefully if anything is being reused
- Open walls/floor only as needed
- Inspect for water damage or hidden issues

### 3. Rough-In / Prep

- Plumbing adjustments
- Electrical and lighting updates
- Ventilation upgrades if needed
- Wall repair or replacement
- Shower waterproofing system
- Floor prep and leveling if needed

### 4. Tile and Finish Work

- Floor tile installation
- Shower tile installation
- Grout
- Paint
- Install sinks, faucets, hardware, mirrors, and lights

### 5. Final Review

- Check for leaks
- Confirm drainage and shower slope
- Seal anything that requires sealing
- Touch up paint and caulk
- Final clean
- Add final photos and notes
