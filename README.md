## Current Status

**Phase:** Planning / design direction  
**Main goal:** Full bathroom refresh with a lighter, cleaner, timeless look while evaluating whether to keep or replace the existing black granite vanity top.

## Project Goals

- Create a bathroom that feels lighter, bigger, airy, modern, and timeless
- Compare keeping the existing black granite countertop against a practical remnant replacement
- Replace or update everything else: sinks, faucets, mirrors, lighting, tile, walls, shower finishes, and hardware
- Use finishes that are durable, easy to clean, and not overly trendy
- Document all measurements, products, prices, decisions, and progress
- Track budget and schedule before demo begins

## Current Design Direction

### Overall Style

Current target style:

- Bright, airy, and clean
- Soft minimal / Scandinavian-inspired
- Hotel-like, but not cold
- Timeless but modern
- White, ivory, light greige, and soft neutral colors
- Simple lines, light materials, and calm finishes
- Avoid anything that looks too plastic, cheap, busy, or overly trendy

## Vanity Countertop Conflict

The current plan contains conflicting countertop directions that require clarification:

- Compare retaining the existing black granite with replacing it using a quartz or granite remnant
- Quartz is the preferred replacement material if the countertop is replaced
- A newer design update says to keep the existing black granite countertop and not modify its color
- The granite backsplash can be removed if it improves the final design because it is not permanently fixed to the countertop.

### Vanity Layout

- Keep the general double-vanity layout.
- Use vessel sinks that sit on top of the existing countertop because of the existing cutouts.
- Preferred sink color: white.
- Preferred sink shape: rectangular or softly squared, not round.

## Vanity / Countertop Area Decisions

### Cabinet

Current decision:

- Cabinet should be white.
- Style should stay clean and timeless, similar to shaker-style doors/drawers.
- The white cabinet keeps the vanity bright so the black granite countertop feels intentional instead of heavy.

### Hardware

Current decision:

- Use matte black hardware throughout.
- This includes vanity pulls, knobs, faucets, mirror frames, towel rings, shower glass hardware, shower valve trim, shower head, handheld sprayer, and drain cover if available.
- Black hardware works with the black granite countertop and helps tie the vanity and shower together.

Design note:

- Black hardware is modern-classic rather than the most traditional timeless finish.
- It should work well here because the countertop is already black.
- Keep the room very bright everywhere else so the black accents feel balanced and intentional.

### Mirrors

Current direction:

- Use tall mirrors with simple black frames.
- Rounded rectangle mirrors are preferred because they soften the room while staying modern.
- **Mandatory:** Include diffused light behind the mirrors. The mirror/electrical plan must support a soft, even backlit glow behind the mirrors.

### Vanity Lighting

Current decision:

- Use black horizontal bar lights above the mirrors.
- Keep the lights simple, modern, and minimal.
- Use warm white lighting, approximately 2700K–3000K.
- Avoid overly decorative fixtures.
- Diffused mirror backlighting is mandatory and should be treated as part of the vanity lighting plan, not an optional upgrade.

## Shower Construction Requirements

- Proper waterproofing behind the tile
- Correct shower-floor slope so water drains completely
- Sound, level subfloor and wall framing before the shower is rebuilt
- Good ventilation to reduce moisture, mildew, and cleaning issues
- Durable grout selection
- Easy-to-clean finishes

### Shower Reconstruction Plan

Waterproofing is the most important part of the shower remodel. The finished shower must prevent leaks, trapped moisture, mold, and damage to the bathroom floor or the rest of the house.

#### 1. Demolish and Inspect

- Demolish the existing shower and remove the unwanted granite bench
- Open the planned wall and expose the shower framing
- Pause before rebuilding to inspect all hidden conditions
- Check the wall insulation and replace it if it is old, damaged, damp, moldy, or inadequate
- Inspect framing for rot, mold, water damage, movement, or anything out of square
- Inspect the shower subfloor / wood decking after the bench is removed
- Repair or replace damaged subfloor and add support where the removed bench or new layout requires it
- Confirm that plumbing, drain placement, and the planned shower layout will work before closing walls

#### 2. Rebuild the Shower Structure

- Complete any necessary framing, plumbing, insulation, and subfloor repairs
- Make walls flat, plumb, and square enough for the selected wall system and tile
- Make the shower base solid and level before creating the required drainage slope
- Build a properly sloped shower floor so water flows to the drain without pooling
- Confirm whether a curb, curbless entry, niche, shelf, or other feature changes the waterproofing plan

#### 3. Install One Complete Waterproofing System

- Select a proven shower waterproofing system before purchasing materials
- Use compatible components from the selected system for the walls, shower base, drain connection, corners, seams, fasteners, niches, curb, and pipe penetrations
- Follow the manufacturer's installation instructions exactly, including required thicknesses, overlaps, cure times, and approved sealants
- Do not rely on tile, grout, or caulk as the primary waterproof barrier
- Avoid mixing incompatible products or methods unless the manufacturers explicitly approve it

#### 4. Verify Before Tiling

- Inspect every seam, corner, penetration, transition, and drain connection
- Perform the required shower-pan flood test before installing tile
- Confirm the waterproofed shower floor still slopes correctly toward the drain
- Correct every leak, low spot, or waterproofing defect before covering the system
- Photograph the completed waterproofing and test results for project records

#### 5. Tile and Final Leak Check

- Install tile, grout, and movement-joint sealant using products compatible with the waterproofing system
- Confirm the finished shower floor drains fully without standing water
- Check plumbing connections and the completed shower for leaks
- Maintain ventilation, grout, and sealant so the shower remains dry and mold-resistant

## Bathroom Floor Decisions

Current direction:

- Use large-format bathroom floor tiles.
- Do not use small mosaic tile for the main bathroom floor.
- Floor should be light, bright, and airy.
- Preferred tones: warm ivory, light greige, soft beige, or light stone-look porcelain.
- Matte or low-sheen finish preferred for safety and a softer look.
- Minimal grout lines where possible.

Design note:

- It is common to match the shower wall tile and bathroom floor tile, but the current preferred direction is to coordinate them instead of making everything exactly the same.
- Coordinating materials should make the room feel connected without looking flat or like a showroom box.

## Shower Design Decisions

### Overall Shower Look

Current direction:

- We love the current shower design direction.
- Keep the shower bright, clean, modern, and spa-like.
- Use a frameless glass shower enclosure.
- Use matte black shower hardware to match the vanity area.
- Remove the existing granite shower seat / bench if possible.
- Avoid finishes that look like plastic.

### Shower Wall Tile

Current decision:

- Use large-format bright porcelain tiles on the shower walls.
- Preferred look: soft white or warm ivory porcelain.
- Finish should be matte or satin, not overly glossy.
- Pattern should be subtle, such as light stone movement or soft marble-like veining.
- Avoid busy or high-contrast veining.

### Shower Floor Tile

Current direction:

- Shower floor should coordinate with the shower walls and bathroom floor.
- Because of slope and grip, use a smaller tile or mosaic on the shower floor.
- Keep the color family light and soft.
- Porcelain mosaic remains the preferred shower floor direction.

### Shower Niche

Current direction:

- Include a long recessed shower niche.
- Add subtle built-in lighting if practical.
- Keep the niche clean and minimal.

### Shower Hardware

Current decision:

- Use matte black shower hardware.
- Black frameless glass hardware / rail.
- Black shower head.
- Black valve trim.
- Black handheld sprayer.
- Black drain cover if available.

## Material Coordination Strategy

The current plan is to coordinate materials instead of matching everything exactly.

Recommended approach:

- Shower walls: large bright porcelain tile in soft white or warm ivory.
- Bathroom floor: large-format light greige, warm ivory, soft beige, or light stone-look porcelain.
- Shower floor: smaller mosaic in the same general color family for grip and slope.
- Vanity: white cabinet with existing black granite countertop.
- Sinks: white vessel sinks.
- Hardware: matte black throughout.
- Lighting: black horizontal bar lights above mirrors plus mandatory diffused light behind the mirrors.

This should create one connected style while keeping the bathroom bright, airy, modern, and timeless.

## Project Phases

### 1. Planning

- Confirm design direction
- Take measurements
- Select materials
- Confirm budget
- Decide what work is DIY vs contractor
- Order long-lead items

### 2. Demo

- Empty bathroom
- Protect surrounding areas
- Remove old fixtures, mirrors, lighting, and finishes
- Remove vanity items carefully if anything is being reused
- Demolish the shower, remove the existing bench, and open the planned wall
- Inspect exposed insulation, framing, plumbing, and subfloor
- Document water damage, mold, rot, or hidden issues before rebuilding

### 3. Rough-In / Prep

- Plumbing adjustments
- Electrical and lighting updates, including wiring/placement for mandatory diffused mirror backlighting
- Ventilation upgrades if needed
- Replace inadequate or damaged insulation
- Repair framing, walls, and subfloor as needed
- Prepare flat, plumb walls and a solid, level shower base
- Build the required shower-floor slope
- Install the complete shower waterproofing system
- Flood-test and inspect the waterproofing before tile
- Prepare and level the main bathroom floor if needed

### 4. Tile and Finish Work

- Floor tile installation
- Shower tile installation
- Grout
- Paint
- Install sinks, faucets, hardware, mirrors, and lights

### 5. Final Review

- Check for leaks
- Confirm drainage and shower slope
- Seal anything that requires sealing
- Touch up paint and caulk
- Final clean
- Add final photos and notes
